Initiatives

Some of the initiatives taken by the Indian Government towards women enpowerment and entrepreneurship

Beti Bachao, Beti Padhao (Save the Daughter, Educate the Daughter) Scheme (India)

Launched in 2015, this initiative aims to address gender-based discrimination and promote the value of girls in Indian society. It focuses on improving the declining child sex ratio, enhancing access to education for girls, and creating awareness about the importance of gender equality. The scheme includes measures such as cash incentives for girl child education, community outreach programs, and advocacy campaigns to change societal attitudes towards the girl child.

Mahila Shakti Kendra (Women Power Centers)

Introduced as part of the National Rural Livelihood Mission, the Mahila Shakti Kendra initiative aims to empower rural women by providing them with support services, skill development training, and access to resources. These centers serve as hubs for women's empowerment activities, offering services such as vocational training, entrepreneurship development, health and nutrition education, and legal awareness programs. By strengthening women's capacities and promoting their participation in decision-making processes, the initiative contributes to their socio-economic empowerment and upliftment.

SheTrades Initiative (Global)

Led by the International Trade Centre (ITC), the SheTrades Initiative seeks to connect three million women-owned businesses to international markets by 2021. It provides women entrepreneurs with access to training, networking opportunities, and resources to help them grow their businesses and expand their presence in global trade. The initiative focuses on addressing the specific challenges faced by women in accessing markets, finance, and information, with the aim of unlocking the full potential of women entrepreneurs to drive economic growth and sustainable development worldwide.

Women's Entrepreneurship Development Network

WEDN is an initiative of the World Bank Group that supports women entrepreneurs in developing countries. It offers capacity-building programs, access to finance, and networking opportunities to help women start and grow their businesses. WEDN focuses on addressing the unique challenges faced by women entrepreneurs, such as limited access to finance, markets, and business networks, and works to create an enabling environment for women's entrepreneurship through policy advocacy and stakeholder engagement.

Global Fund for Women:

The Global Fund for Women is an international NGO that supports women's rights organizations and movements around the world. While not exclusively focused on entrepreneurship, the organization funds projects that empower women economically, including initiatives that support women-owned businesses and promote women's access to economic opportunities. The Global Fund for Women provides grants, capacity-building support, and advocacy resources to women's organizations working to advance gender equality and women's rights globally.
